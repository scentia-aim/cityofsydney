Cheat Sheet 2

AI in
Plain English

Keep it simple.
Build confidence.

  • AI — software that learns patterns from data
  • Generative AI — creates new text, images, code
  • Prompt — the instruction you give the AI
  • Context — background that improves the result
  • Hallucination — AI confidently makes things up
  • Workflow — repeatable AI-assisted steps
  • Human review — you always check the output

Cheat Sheet 5

Bad Prompt → Better Prompt

BAD PROMPTS

  • "Write an email"
  • "Summarise this"
  • "Help with my business"

BETTER PROMPTS

  • Include role, context, goal, tone and format
  • Specify what to focus on and for whom
  • Name the specific problem and constraints
Activity — 4 minutes

Upgrade a Weak Prompt

Your improved prompt must include:

  • Role — who should the AI act as?
  • Context — what is the situation?
  • Task — what exactly do you need?
  • Tone — how should it sound?
  • Format — what shape should the output take?

ChatGPT The versatile all-rounder
WritingCodingDataDocumentsImagesDeep ResearchAgents
PracticalFlexibleMainstreamFastVersatile

Strengths

  • Best overall “Swiss Army knife” — turns messy requests into useful outputs
  • Strong at documents, coding, images, structured tables and business workflows
  • Agent mode completes multi-step tasks and can be guided or interrupted mid-task

Watch-outs

  • Versatility can lead to over-trust — outputs still need checking
  • Advanced features vary by subscription tier
  • Model names and available modes change frequently
Reach for ChatGPT when you need the best general-purpose AI assistant for business work.
gemini.google
Gemini The Google-connected research & productivity assistant
ResearchGmailDrivePlanningPresentationsBrainstorming
OrganisedSearch-awareIntegratedPractical

Strengths

  • Strong fit for organisations already using Google Workspace
  • Deep Research draws from Gmail, Drive, Chat and the web
  • Canvas turns research into apps, quizzes, web pages and infographics

Watch-outs

  • Best value depends on how much your business lives inside Google
  • Can feel more “productivity tool” than conversationally warm
  • Outputs still need human checking, especially for customer-facing content
Reach for Gemini when your work lives in Google and you want AI connected to research, documents and productivity.
claude.ai
Claude The careful writer and long-document thinker
Long docsWritingAnalysisPolicyCodingStrategy
ThoughtfulCarefulPolishedMeasured

Strengths

  • Excellent for long, careful writing with strong tone and nuance
  • Summarises and reasons across lengthy materials with up to 1M tokens of context
  • Claude Cowork integrates into desktop workflows on macOS and Windows

Watch-outs

  • Can be more cautious than some users expect
  • Less ideal for very short, punchy, high-volume outputs
  • Feature availability depends on plan and product surface
Reach for Claude when the work is long, sensitive, complex or needs careful writing.
grok.com
Grok The real-time, internet-aware assistant
Real-timeTrendsX / SocialCodingDocumentsLive Search
FastDirectOpinionatedInternet-native

Strengths

  • Strong for up-to-date web and X-aware insights with live search
  • Useful for social listening, trend spotting and current-context work
  • 1M token context window with strong agentic tool calling

Watch-outs

  • Less neutral “enterprise” personality — can feel more provocative or informal
  • X-based context can be noisy, biased or volatile
  • Needs careful verification for sensitive business, legal or brand work
Reach for Grok when freshness, social trends and real-time internet context matter most.

Before You Paste:
The Safe AI Checklist

Never paste confidential, personal, financial or sensitive information into AI tools unless your organisation has approved systems and policies.

Protect your data

How to Turn Off
Training

Most AI tools train on your conversations by default. Go into the settings of your chosen AI model and turn off training.

Settings → Data Controls → Improve the model
Settings → Gemini Apps Activity → Turn off
Does not train on conversations by default
Settings → Data Sharing → Turn off

Section 10

Introduction to
AI Agents

Chat — a one-off conversation for quick answers and drafts
Workflow — a saved, repeatable sequence of prompts you run regularly
Agent — an AI that can take actions autonomously: browse the web, use tools, complete multi-step tasks

Most people are still at the chat stage — understanding the progression helps you plan ahead.

Agents today

What Can Agents
Do Today?

  • Research a topic across multiple sources and produce a structured report
  • Monitor inboxes, summarise messages, and draft replies
  • Schedule meetings, manage calendars, send follow-ups
  • Automate repetitive data entry, file organisation and reporting

Agents still need human oversight — you set the goal, review the output, and own the result.

The near future

Where Agents
Are Heading

  • Claude, ChatGPT and Gemini are rapidly adding agent capabilities
  • Small businesses will soon delegate routine admin, customer follow-up and reporting to AI agents
  • The key skill: learning to write clear instructions and set boundaries for what the agent can and cannot do

Section 11 · Cheat Sheet 6

Trust, But Check

AI can be confidently wrong. Always check: names, dates, numbers, sources, legal claims, financial claims, customer promises, and anything that sounds too perfect.

Cheat Sheet 7

Would This Be Fair
to Everyone?

Before applying AI to staff, customers, policies or public communications, ask:

  • Could this exclude someone?
  • Could it stereotype or generalise unfairly?
  • Could it favour one group over another?
  • Could it cause harm if taken at face value?
