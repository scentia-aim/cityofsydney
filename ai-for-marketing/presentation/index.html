Safety first

Safe AI
for Marketing

Never paste into AI tools:

  • Private customer data — names, emails, purchase history
  • Financial details — revenue, margins, pricing strategy
  • Staff information — salaries, performance, personal details
  • Confidential strategy — unreleased plans, partnerships, IP

Use generic examples. Keep real data out of prompts.

Prompting

Act — Explain — Please

Act as the expert (social media manager, copywriter, strategist)
Explain your business, audience and goal
Please ask for the specific output + format

Better prompting

Bad Prompt → Better Prompt

WEAK PROMPTS

  • "Make a post"
  • "Write me an email"
  • "Create a campaign"

BETTER PROMPTS INCLUDE

  • Role — who should the AI act as?
  • Audience — who are you talking to?
  • Goal — what action do you want?
  • Platform — where will this appear?
  • Tone — how should it sound?

Your secret weapon

The Marketing
Prompt Formula

Role + Business + Audience + Goal + Channel + Tone + Format

"Act as a social media strategist for a Sydney boutique targeting millennial women. Write three Instagram carousel captions for our winter sale in a warm, playful tone. Include CTAs and hashtags."

  • Wants — what they're looking for

Activity — 5 minutes

Build Your
Customer Persona

Create one customer avatar for your business:

  • Give them a name and a short description
  • What do they want from your business?
  • What worries or frustrations do they have?
  • Where do they spend time online?
  • What would make them buy or book today?

Output: a reusable persona paragraph you can paste into any prompt.

Brand voice

Sound Like Us

Brand voice is the filter for every AI output. Without it, AI writes generic content that sounds like everyone else.

  • Pick 3 words that describe how your brand sounds
  • Choose words you never use
  • Add an example sentence that nails your tone
  • Paste this block into every marketing prompt
Activity — 5 minutes

Create Your
Brand Voice Block

Write a reusable brand voice prompt block:

  • 3 words that describe your tone (e.g. warm, witty, professional)
  • 1–2 words you never use
  • One example sentence that sounds exactly like you
Template: "Our brand voice is [word, word, word]. We never say [X]. An example of our tone: [sentence]."

Quality control

Avoid AI Slop

AI without brand voice produces content that is:

  • Generic — could be for any business
  • Vague — no real detail or specifics
  • Robotic — unnatural sentence patterns
  • Cliché-heavy — "leverage", "unlock", "game-changer"
  • Overexcited — too many exclamation marks!!!

The fix:

  • Always include your persona
  • Always include your brand voice block
  • Always review before publishing

Review management

Review Reply
Cheat Sheet

  • Stay calm — never reply when angry or defensive
  • Be professional — short, empathetic, solution-focused
  • Protect privacy — never share customer details in public replies
  • Thank positives — brief, genuine, specific
  • Address negatives — acknowledge, apologise, offer to resolve privately

Pre-publish checklist

Ready to Publish?

  • Accuracy — are all facts, dates and prices correct?
  • Brand voice — does it sound like us?
  • CTA — is there a clear call to action?
  • Links — do all links work and go to the right place?
  • Rights — do we have permission for all images?
  • Mobile — does it look good on a phone?

Responsible AI

Trust, But Check

AI can be confidently wrong. Before publishing, always verify:

  • Offers and prices — are they current and accurate?
  • Claims — can you back them up?
  • Testimonials — are they real?
  • Dates and events — are they correct?
  • Legal language — have you checked with a professional?

Ethics & inclusion

Would This Be Fair
to Everyone?

Before publishing AI-generated marketing content, ask:

  • Could this stereotype a group of people?
  • Could it exclude part of our audience?
  • Does it represent our community fairly?
  • Is the imagery accessible (alt text, contrast, readability)?
